Chocolate Peanut Butter Banana Protein Shake

A Deliciously Sweet, Milkshake-Like Protein Shake

This concoction will bring all of the boys to the yard

Who doesn't love an ice-cold milkshake (excluding the lactose-intolerant among us)? I'll tell you who: sociopaths and moral degenerates.

Jokes aside, anyone who has eaten at a diner or any diner-adjacent restaurant has most likely had a milkshake in their life and who can blame them? When you're going to town on that burger and shoving french fries in your gullet, what's more appealing than washing it all down with a thick and sweet milkshake? Not much, to be quite honest.

Unfortunately, milkshakes are calorie-bombs of a magnitude so great, that they would make the foremost minds of the Manhattan Project cower in fear. What can you expect when you mix ice cream, full-fat milk, sugar, and whole bunch of other sweet confections? What makes milkshakes particularly troublesome for the waistline is the fact that they're mostly liquid: liquid foods are nowhere nearly as satiating as solid foods. You can easily chug an 800-calorie milkshake in 10 seconds flat and it will do next to nothing to satisfy your hunger. This, on top of the fact that milkshakes are primarily consumed in conjunction with high-calorie foods like hamburgers and french fries, makes milkshakes something you definitely should avoid if you are watching your weight.

What if you could make something taste just as good as a milkshake, in the comfort of your own home, with a fraction of the calories? I've got the solution. By making a couple of sensible substitutions to the traditional milkshake, we can have a thick and creamy beverage that can actually be considered pretty darn nutritious.

Ingredients

  • 1 frozen banana
  • 1 cup of nonfat/skim milk
  • 1 scoop of chocolate-flavored whey protein powder
  • 1 teaspoon of natural peanut butter

A couple of healthy substitutions goes a long way. Compared to a traditional milkshake, this concoction will contain much more protein, less fat, and fewer calories. We completely omit ice cream, since there's absolutely no room for something like ice cream in a healthy recipe. Skim milk is used in favor of whole milk to save on the calories; we're already getting some fat calories from the little bit of peanut butter we're adding.

It also important that the banana be frozen for this recipe: this makes the smoothie/shake much more frothy and creamy than if you were to put a regular banana to the blender.

Steps

  1. Add 1 cup of nonfat/skim dairy milk to a blender

    2. Putting the liquid first is important, since it makes the blending process smoother and enables easier clean-up afterwards.

  2. Add 1 frozen banana to the blender. For best results, break the banana into several, smaller pieces before adding.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of natural peanut butter. Try not to get any on the sides of the blender: this will make it harder to clean later.
  4. Add 1 scoop of chocolate whey protein powder
  5. Blend the ingredients until thick and creamy.

    6. Depending on your blender's size and speed, this may take anywhere from 30 seconds to a minute.

  6. Enjoy!

And there you have it! A much healthier alternative to the universally-appreciated milkshake. This protein shake is nutritious, delicious, and oh-so-sweet!
